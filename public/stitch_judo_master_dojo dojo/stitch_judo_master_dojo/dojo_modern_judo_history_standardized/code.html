Origins & Evolution

The Gentle Way

From the battlefields of feudal Japan to the Olympic podium: the story of Jigoro Kano's revolutionary vision.

Judo is more than a sport; it is a philosophy of maximum efficiency and mutual welfare. Born from the ancient combat system of Jiu-jitsu, it was refined into a pedagogical system by a young scholar named Jigoro Kano in 1882.

“
"It is not important to be better than someone else, but to be better than yesterday."
— KANO JIGORO, FOUNDER OF JUDO

The Shift from 'Jutsu' to 'Do'

Traditional Jiu-jitsu focused on the 'Jutsu' (art/technique) of lethal combat. Kano introduced 'Do' (the way), pivoting from killing techniques to a method of physical and mental cultivation.

The Kodokan

Started with only nine mats in a small Buddhist temple, the Kodokan (Place for Expounding the Way) became the world headquarters for modern Judo.
