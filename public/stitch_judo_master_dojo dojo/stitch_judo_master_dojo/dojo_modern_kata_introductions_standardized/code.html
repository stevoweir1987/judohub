lightbulb The Purpose of Kata

While Randori (free practice) focuses on application, Kata focuses on the "perfect form." It allows practitioners to study high-level techniques safely, including those too dangerous for competition, ensuring the full technical spectrum of Judo is never lost.

Sensei's Tip

"Kata is not just a performance; it is a live conversation between Tori and Uke."

Nage-no-kata

Forms of Throwing
sports_martial_arts

Comprising 15 fundamental throwing techniques, this Kata is divided into five sets: Te-waza, Koshi-waza, Ashi-waza, Ma-sutemi-waza, and Yoko-sutemi-waza.

LEVEL: ADVANCED

Katame-no-kata

Forms of Grappling
hub

Focuses on the three pillars of Newaza: Osaekomi-waza (pins), Shime-waza (strangles), and Kansetsu-waza (joint locks). A masterclass in control and pressure.

YIELDING & HARMONY

Ju-no-kata

The "Forms of Gentleness." Unlike others, this is performed without throwing, emphasizing the principle of 'Ju' (yielding) to an opponent's force to gain victory.
