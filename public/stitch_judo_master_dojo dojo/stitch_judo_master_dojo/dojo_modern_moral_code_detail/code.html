COURTESY

REI • れい

menu_book The Philosophy

In the context of the Judo Moral Code, Courtesy is the fundamental beginning and end of all martial practice. It is not merely polite behavior, but a deep-seated respect for oneself, one's opponent, and the art itself.

Without courtesy, Judo ceases to be a way of life and becomes merely a method of combat. To practice Rei is to acknowledge the humanity in others, ensuring that every interaction in the Dojo—and in life—is rooted in dignity and mutual benefit (Jita Kyoei).

Practical Application

  • check_circle Bowing correctly when entering or leaving the mat.
  • check_circle Maintaining personal hygiene as a sign of respect to your partner.
  • check_circle Active listening when an instructor or peer is speaking.
