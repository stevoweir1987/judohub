The Legacy of Jigoro Kano

The Visionary

Judo was founded by Jigoro Kano, a brilliant educator and athlete who transformed ancient Jujutsu into a modern system of physical, mental, and moral education. Seeking a way to make martial arts safer and more pedagogical, he focused on the principle of Seiryoku Zenyo (Maximum Efficiency, Minimum Effort).

"Judo is the way to the most effective use of both physical and spiritual power."

— Jigoro Kano
history

Historical Milestones

1882

Founding of the Kodokan

Kano establishes the Kodokan Judo Institute at Eisho-ji Temple with only nine disciples and twelve mats. This marks the transition from 'Jutsu' (art) to 'Do' (way).

1886

The Tokyo Police Tournament

In a landmark challenge against traditional Jujutsu schools, Kodokan practitioners won decisively, proving the technical superiority and safety of Kano's refined methods.

1964

Olympic Recognition

Judo makes its debut at the Summer Olympics in Tokyo, solidifying its status as a premier global sport and fulfilling Kano's dream of international recognition.

The Core Philosophy

Seiryoku Zenyo

Maximum efficiency with minimum effort. Using the opponent's strength against them to achieve victory.

Jita Kyoei

Mutual welfare and benefit. Training not just for self-improvement, but for the betterment of society.
