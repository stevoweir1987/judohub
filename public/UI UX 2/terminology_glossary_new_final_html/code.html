Core Principles

FOUNDATION info

Kuzushi

[koo-zoo-shee]

The breaking of balance. In learning, it represents disrupting existing mental models to create space for new knowledge acquisition.

POSITIONING info

Tsukuri

[tsoo-koo-ree]

Entering and positioning. The precise alignment of resources and focus required to execute a complex cognitive task effectively.

EXECUTION info

Kake

[kah-keh]

The completion of the technique. The final execution phase where theory transforms into practical application and mastery.

Technique Modifiers

O [oh]

Major or Large. Applied to comprehensive, high-level strategies.

Ko [koh]

Minor or Small. Focuses on granular details and micro-adjustments.

Modifier Logic

Learn how directional and magnitude-based modifiers reshape the core principles of the Dojo practice.

Uchi [oo-chee]

Inner or Inside. Internalized habits and mental cognition.

Soto [soh-toh]

Outer or Outside. External feedback and peer interactions.

Gari [gah-ree]

Reaping motion. In learning, the active harvesting of insights from complex data sets.

Gake [gah-keh]

Hooking or Entangling. The process of anchoring new concepts to established mental structures.
